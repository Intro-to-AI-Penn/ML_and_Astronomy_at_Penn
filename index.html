Machine Learning in
Astronomy @ Penn

Researchers at Penn are active in analyzing data from surveys of stars and galaxies, containing images of ~1 billion objects, and maps of other continuous fields like the temperature of the cosmic background radiation. They create and use machine learning algorithms to deblend galaxy images, classify different types of astronomical objects, and constrain our cosmological models from maps of the distribution of the dark matter in the universe.

Faculty Research Groups

Prof. Bhuv Jain

Research in Prof. Jain’s group focuses on cosmology and weak gravitational lensing, using galaxy images to build maps of the dark matter distribution, develop clustering statistics, and infer cosmological model parameters. Topics include using neural networks to deblend galaxies in astronomical images, finding the combination of cosmological parameters that can be optimally constrained from combinations of datasets using normalizing flows, and extracting cosmological information from gravitational lensing mass maps using Convolutional Neural Networks. Current activities of the group include investigating the use of Bayesian Neural Networks for cosmological parameter inference and the application of Transformer-based models in astrophysics.

Prof. Masao Sako

Prof. Sako’s group focuses on time-domain astronomy: identification and classification of phenomena in time-series data. The group has worked on a variety of problems across the full data-processing pipeline, including transient detection, identifying and classifying supernovae, as well as parameter inference using Gaussian processes and convolutional neural networks.

Prof. Mariangela Bernardi

Prof. Bernardi’s group uses deep learning techniques to estimate galaxy morphologies directly from the image pixels in galaxy surveys. Current activities include exploring how best to transfer learning from one survey to another.
